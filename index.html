Why I started this

We don't live in the world where the Maharaja sponsors the yogi.

We live in a real economy, as householders, with rent and health insurance and people who depend on us. So we have to embrace the tools it takes to make a living doing what we love — and right now, the biggest of those tools is AI.

Business isn't a dirty word. Business is taking the skills you've earned — empathy, holding space, a beginner's mind — and offering them to someone who needs them, in exchange for being able to keep doing the work. Those are the same skills that make great marketing. You already have them.

Here's the hard truth I shared on the podcast: the teachers who use these tools are going to pass by the ones who don't — the same way a website passed the business card. But yoga itself? That stays human. Healing comes through connection, not through wires. AI won't replace you. It'll just help the right people find you.

What I believe this network is for

  • Put the business side first — because that's what actually gets you work
  • Give every teacher the AI and marketing tools that used to cost a team
  • Build real demand — clinics, schools, hotels, workplaces — and route it to you
  • Prove what yoga does, together, with our own research
  • Keep the human at the center; the tech just clears the path

A teacher's story · how the network helps, in practice

Satyavathi spent years in IT. Then she chose the mat.

Satyavathi Devi knows the exact stress your team carries, because she carried it too — years in the IT industry before she trained as a yoga teacher and yoga therapist. Today she runs The Luminine in Princeton, New Jersey: stress-relief yoga, breathwork, Yin, Yoga Nidra, chair yoga and Face Yoga, built for real, screen-heavy lives.

She's exactly who I started this for — a gifted teacher who didn't want to spend her evenings learning SEO, cold-email and CRM software just to find clients.

🎯

What she needed

A way to reach the big employers around her — pharma, insurance, research — without selling "yoga classes" to skeptical HR buyers, and without a marketing team she couldn't afford.

🧩

What the network built

An SEO microsite to rank for "corporate yoga New Jersey," a measured 8-week program with an HR-readable scorecard, a CRM and welcome sequence to capture leads, and a personalized outreach list for the Route 1 corridor.

🌱

What changed

She sells outcomes, not classes — "less stress, sharper focus" — backed by a before/after read any HR lead can defend. The pipeline runs while she does what she's great at: teaching.

Questions, answered

Frequently asked

Who is My Yoga Network for?

Anyone who loves yoga and wants to see it reach more people — but especially yoga teachers and yoga therapists who want to make a real living doing the work they love. If you teach, train, or want to bring yoga into clinics, schools, hotels or workplaces, this is built for you.

Is it free to join?

Yes. Joining the community as a teacher is free. You can use the chatbot, ask questions, and add yourself to the teacher database so we can match you with work. Paid programs and agency services are optional.

Will AI replace yoga teachers?

No. Yoga is a human connection — healing comes through people, not wires. But teachers who use AI to find clients and run their business will move faster than those who don't. This network exists to put those tools in your hands, not to replace you.

What kind of work does the network help me get?

We connect teachers and therapists with demand we're already building: hotels, schools, corporate and workplace wellness, and clinical/healthcare partners — and we share the marketing, outreach and AI tools so you can build your own pipeline too.
